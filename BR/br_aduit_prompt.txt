Definitions:

label_match:
- true if category == current_label.
- false if category != current_label.

confirmed_label:
- Must be identical to category.
- This is the label the audit juror confirms according to the BR taxonomy.

usable:
- true if the sample is suitable as generated training data under confirmed_label.
- false if the sample is refusal/meta text, low-quality synthetic text, clearly wrong for the generated supplement, too ambiguous, or unsuitable for training use.

risk_level:
- low: current_label is clearly correct and the sample is suitable.
- medium: current_label may be correct, but the sample has boundary ambiguity, template artifacts, source leakage, operational detail, weak realism, or needs human review.
- high: current_label is incorrect, the sample clearly belongs to another category, the sample is refusal/meta text, or the sample is unsuitable for the generated supplement.

audit_action:
- accept: use only when usable=true, label_match=true, and risk_level is low.
- review: use when usable may be true but risk_level is medium, or when confidence is below 0.75.
- reject: use when usable=false, risk_level is high, the sample is refusal/meta text, or the sample is unsuitable for generated supplement.

Important audit rules:
- Independently classify the text first. Use current_label only after classification.
- Do not modify the dataset. If category differs from current_label, output the correct category as confirmed_label, set label_match=false, and choose audit_action based on whether the sample is usable training data under confirmed_label.
- Do not use safe for uncertainty. If an unsafe reading is supported by the text, choose the closest unsafe category.
- Do not penalize a sample merely because it is offensive or unsafe; this is violation training data. Penalize it only if it does not fit current_label, has bad boundary quality, is unrealistic/template-like, or is unsuitable for the generated supplement.
- For CSAM or non-consensual sexual content, follow the BR policy category. Do not invent a separate category.
- For financial fraud, Pix theft, banking fraud, phishing for money, scams, or deceptive impersonation, follow the BR policy: Dangerous_Content, not Cybersecurity_Malware.
- For spam abuse, SEO spam, fake traffic, fake engagement, bulk account abuse, gray-market automation, phishing pages/kits/flows, credential theft, token/cookie theft, CAPTCHA bypass, 2FA bypass, account takeover, and session hijacking, follow the BR policy: Cybersecurity_Malware.
- For Brazil-specific democratic order, TSE/STF/Congresso, election legitimacy, military intervention, coup support, AI-5 restoration, Three Powers attacks, or criminal factions challenging state authority, follow the BR policy: BR_State_Security_Democratic_Order.
