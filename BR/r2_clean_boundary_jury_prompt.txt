You are auditing Brazil (BR) content-safety training data for label-boundary cleanup.

Your job is NOT to make the dataset larger. Your job is to decide whether one
existing LLM-generated sample should be kept, relabeled, or dropped.

Return exactly one JSON object and no extra text:
{
  "action": "keep" | "relabel" | "drop",
  "correct_category": "Dangerous_Content" | "Harassment" | "Hate_Speech" | "Sexually_Explicit_Information" | "Politically_Sensitive_Topics" | "Cybersecurity_Malware" | "BR_State_Security_Democratic_Order" | "safe",
  "confidence": 0.0,
  "reasoning": "brief reason"
}

Allowed labels:
- Dangerous_Content
- Harassment
- Hate_Speech
- Sexually_Explicit_Information
- Politically_Sensitive_Topics
- Cybersecurity_Malware
- BR_State_Security_Democratic_Order
- safe

Input fields:
- current_category: the label currently assigned in the dataset
- text: the original text

Brazil label rules for this cleanup:

1. Dangerous_Content overrides Cybersecurity_Malware when the cyber behavior
directly enables financial fraud, banking/payment theft, Pix scams, asset theft,
phishing for money, bank/payment credential theft, 2FA theft for payment/banking,
or deceptive impersonation for financial gain.

Examples that should be Dangerous_Content, not Cybersecurity_Malware:
- fake Nubank/Caixa/Banco do Brasil/Bradesco/Itaú/Santander login page
- PayPal/Mercado Pago/Mercado Livre phishing kit with 2FA/cookie/token capture
- Pix scam automation or phishing for bank credentials
- stealing payment account sessions or bank cookies

Cybersecurity_Malware should be used for pure cyber abuse such as malware,
ransomware, DDoS, SQL injection, XSS, exploit scripts, unauthorized access,
credential/token/cookie theft outside direct financial-asset theft, CAPTCHA/2FA
bypass, account takeover, spam abuse, fake traffic, fake engagement, bulk account
abuse, bot farms, scraping abuse, SEO spam, or cyberattack guidance.

2. safe must be strict in this cleanup. Do not keep as safe if the text contains
suicide/self-harm encouragement or method seeking, scams, phishing, fraud,
serious illegal activity, threats, hate, explicit sexual content, or Brazil
democratic-order redlines.

Examples that should NOT be safe:
- farewell posts suggesting imminent suicide or "I cannot take it anymore"
- "do not force yourself to stay alive" or similar encouragement
- fake prize/refund/payment links or scam promotions
- claims that Brazilian voting machines/TSE stole the election
- calls for military intervention, closing STF/Congress/TSE, coup, or invasion

3. Harassment is for ordinary targeted abuse, humiliation, insults, bullying,
doxing, defamation, weak threats, or non-protected group abuse.

Do not use Harassment when a higher-risk label applies:
- sexualized minors, CSAM, leaked minor content, rape, drugging, hidden cameras,
  revenge porn, or serious sexual abuse -> Dangerous_Content
- credible killing, lynching, severe physical harm, organized violence, or
  explicit attack planning -> Dangerous_Content
- identity-based degradation or protected-group attack -> Hate_Speech
- sexual content focused on arousal rather than insult -> Sexually_Explicit_Information

4. Sexually_Explicit_Information is for consenting adult sexual content, explicit
sexual acts, sexting, adult hookup solicitation, pornographic intent, BDSM tags,
or arousal-focused language.

If sexual words are mainly used as insults, choose Harassment or Hate_Speech.
If sexual content involves minors, coercion, hidden cameras, rape, blackmail, or
non-consensual sharing, choose Dangerous_Content.

5. BR_State_Security_Democratic_Order is Brazil-specific. Use it for unsupported
claims that Brazilian elections, electronic voting machines, vote counting, or
TSE results are fraudulent/stolen/invalid; calls for military intervention,
military coup, closing STF/Congress/TSE, restoring AI-5, invasion/destruction of
democratic institutions, or criminal factions/militias presented as authorities
replacing the state.

Ordinary criticism of Lula, Bolsonaro, STF, TSE, Congress, elections, policy,
corruption, public security, or historical discussion is safe unless a redline
above is triggered.

6. Politically_Sensitive_Topics is for non-Brazil or global political/security
risks such as foreign election fraud rumors, foreign political violence,
destabilizing rumors, coups, wars, public disorder, massacres, or regional
crisis. Brazil democratic-order redlines should use
BR_State_Security_Democratic_Order instead.

Decision policy:
- If current_category is correct and useful, action = "keep".
- If the sample is clearly useful but the label is wrong, action = "relabel".
- If the sample is too ambiguous, template-like, nonsensical, not Brazil-relevant
  where Brazil context is required, or likely to hurt adjacent label boundaries,
  action = "drop".

Be conservative. This cleanup is meant to protect real-data performance, not to
maximize synthetic sample count.
