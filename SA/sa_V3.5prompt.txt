Core Rules:
- Read the original text directly. Do not translate first.
- Preserve slang, dialect, profanity, sexual implication, religious/political reference, hashtag, emoji, romanized term, and euphemism.
- Interpret Arabic dialects, Gulf/Iraqi/Levantine/Egyptian slang, and local cultural signals by context.
- final_label must never be insufficient.
- sample_quality is independent from final_label.
- Use sample_quality: insufficient for short, corrupted, OCR-damaged, ambiguous, culturally implicit, context-dependent, controversial, or unreliable samples.
- sample_quality: insufficient does not mean safe and must not change final_label.

Risk Preference:
This is multilingual/KSA risk annotation. Do not be conservative toward safe.

Safe is the last-resort label.

Do not use safe to express uncertainty.

For short, dialectal, poetic, slang, OCR-damaged, euphemistic, culturally implicit, political, regional-conflict, or locally sensitive text, if any plausible unsafe reading is supported by the text, choose the closest unsafe final_label.

If unsafe evidence exists but the exact unsafe label is uncertain, choose the closest unsafe label and set sample_quality: insufficient.

Do not choose safe for:
insults, contempt, humiliation, threats, hostile collective speech, sexualized abuse, family humiliation, identity-based degradation, tribal/regional/city-origin attacks, violent intimidation, enemy-humiliation poetry, plausible political/security sensitivity, positive or neutral LGBTQ+ content in KSA, Saudi local redline content, or cases where the only reason for safe is difficult Arabic/dialect/culture/OCR/translation.

Label Cards:

Dangerous_Content:
Use for severe physical harm, death, terrorism, violent extremism, weapons, self-harm methods or encouragement, serious illegal activity, regulated-goods trafficking, fraud, phishing, scams, money laundering, asset theft, deceptive impersonation, illegal gambling, smuggling, sexual crimes, non-consensual sexual abuse, CSAM, revenge porn, hidden cameras, voyeurism, drugging, rape, graphic gore, extreme violence, credible violent threats, killing threats, warlike threats, or severe-harm intimidation.
Violence belongs here when severe harm, killing, violent intent, credible violence, weapons, terrorism, or extreme violence is present.

Harassment:
Use for malicious abuse against a specific person or non-protected collective.
Includes direct insults, vulgar abuse, threats, intimidation, humiliation, defamation, bullying, doxing, body-shaming, sexualized insults, family-targeted insults, mother/sister references used to shame a target, implicit sexualized family humiliation, and attacks on companies, schools, fandoms, teams, departments, organizations, or online communities.
Use Harassment for verbal intimidation, revenge threats, hostile humiliation, or weak violent boasting when severe-harm intent is unclear.
Arabic/dialectal abusive terms may include: عير، زب، طيز، كلب، حيوان، خرا، علوق، يا هبله، تفه عليك، يا سالب, or sexual insults involving نكاح, مص, لحس, نيج.

Hate_Speech:
Use for attacks, humiliation, degradation, dehumanization, exclusion, inferiority claims, objectification, sexual degradation, stereotypes, or violence against a protected identity group or a person attacked through protected identity.
Protected identities include nationality, ethnicity, race, tribe, clan, religion, sect, gender, sexual orientation, gender identity, disability, caste, age, veteran status, legally protected identity, regional origin, city-origin, local demonym, and Bedouin/urban-origin identity.
Arabic/KSA group markers such as قبائل, عشيرة, آل, بني, أهل, بدو, حضر, الجداويين, أهل جدة, or similar demonyms indicate protected identity when attacked collectively.
Collective insults, backwardness stereotypes, animalization, social removal, inferiority, or contempt against such groups -> Hate_Speech.
Misogyny, gender-based degradation, women described as commodities, animals, dirty, immoral, unwanted, expired, worthless, disposable, or sexually degraded -> Hate_Speech.

Sexually_Explicit_Information:
Use for pornographic intent, explicit sexual language, sexual acts, arousal-focused content, hookup solicitation, sexual chat, sexual services, genital slang used sexually, sexual role tags, BDSM/sadism/dominance/submission, top/bottom, active/passive, pornographic hashtag clusters, or lewd jokes focused on sexual acts/arousal.
This label does not require an image.
Arabic/dialectal sexual indicators may include: جنس، سكس، نيك، نياج، مص، لحس، عير، زب، طيز، توب، بوتوم، موجب، سالب، فحل، خنيث، ممحون، مشتهي، سادي، مطيع، فرخ.
If sexual words are mainly used to insult or humiliate a target, use Harassment or Hate_Speech.
If sexual content involves minors, coercion, rape, drugging, hidden cameras, voyeurism, revenge porn, sexual extortion, or non-consensual acts, use Dangerous_Content.

Politically_Sensitive_Topics:
Use for political misinformation, political manipulation, destabilizing rumors, public disorder, separatism, rebellion, political violence, violent overthrow, foreign public-figure defamation, coordinated influence operations, election interference, war/conflict sensitivity, massacre claims, refugee-camp violence, unrest, military conflict, terrorism-related political claims, public-security sensitivity, historical-violence claims, or regional crisis.
If political/security sensitivity is plausible but it is unclear whether the subject is Saudi-specific, choose Politically_Sensitive_Topics, not safe.
Clear non-Saudi political content with violence, unrest, public-safety risk, state-security sensitivity, regional conflict, political violence, separatism, rebellion, or destabilizing claims -> Politically_Sensitive_Topics.
Clearly ordinary, low-risk, non-Saudi factual/academic/satirical political discussion can be safe only when unrelated to violence, unrest, war, regime legitimacy, public disorder, mass killing, terrorism, separatism, public-safety risk, regional-security crisis, or another violation.

Cybersecurity_Malware:
Use for malware, ransomware, spyware, trojans, rootkits, DDoS, SQL injection, XSS, exploit scripts, unauthorized scanning/access, credential theft, token/cookie/API-key theft, CAPTCHA bypass, 2FA bypass, account takeover, session hijacking, phishing pages/kits/flows, spam abuse, SEO spam, scraping abuse, fake traffic, fake engagement, bulk account abuse, gray-market automation, or cyberattack guidance.
Benign cybersecurity education or defensive analysis is safe unless it gives actionable abuse guidance.
If cyber behavior directly enables financial fraud, asset theft, phishing for money, money laundering, scams, or deceptive impersonation, use Dangerous_Content.

SA_LGBTQ_Content:
In KSA traffic, use for positive, neutral, supportive, validating, normalizing, de-stigmatizing, identity-sharing, rights-related, or advocacy content about LGBTQ+ people, same-sex relationships/behavior, transgender identity, non-binary identity, bisexuality, asexuality, LGBTQ+ equality/inclusion/anti-discrimination, rainbow flags, symbols, slogans, or community markers.

Positive or neutral LGBTQ+ content in KSA is not safe.
Anti-LGBTQ abuse -> Hate_Speech or Harassment.

SA_Religious_Violation:
In KSA context, use for promoting atheism, agnosticism, materialism, denial of Allah/religion, non-Islamic proselytization, non-Islamic conversion, positive/neutral recommendation of non-Islamic religious gatherings/scriptures/rituals/missionary content, or mockery, insult, questioning, desecration, or degradation of Islam, Allah, Prophet Muhammad, Quran, Hadith, Sharia, Mecca, Medina, Hajj, Islamic law, Islamic rituals, or Islamic sacred concepts.
Normal Islamic teaching, Sunni doctrine, Quran quotation, prayer, Umrah, Hajj, Mecca/Medina visit, or faith renewal is safe.
Attacking religious people as a group -> Hate_Speech.

SA_State_Security_Royalty:
In KSA traffic, use for non-praising discussion, criticism, insult, rumor, sarcasm, questioning, delegitimization, neutral/negative political discussion, jokes, gossip, cartoons, or negative claims about Saudi sovereign entities, Saudi state legitimacy, Saudi religious-political legitimacy, Saudi King, Crown Prince/MBS, Al Saud, core royal family, Hajj/holy-site management by Saudi authorities, Mecca/Medina governance by Saudi authorities, Jamal Khashoggi, 2017 Ritz-Carlton purge, royal infighting, irregular power transition, or Saudi military operations in Yemen / Operation Decisive Storm.
This label removes exemptions for objective criticism, academic discussion, neutral news forwarding, political humor, and satire when the subject is Saudi royal/core state taboo.
Positive praise of Saudi leadership is safe unless another violation is present.
Ordinary complaints about Saudi prices, jobs, traffic, entertainment, services, or daily life are not this label unless they involve leadership, royal family, legitimacy, holy-site management, Mecca/Medina governance, or taboo events.
Saudi-specific redlines may be explicit or strongly implied through local cities, entities, royal names, historical events, political symbols, or regional context.
If political/security sensitivity exists but Saudi-specific evidence is unclear, use Politically_Sensitive_Topics instead of safe.

safe:
Use safe only when the text is understandable enough and no unsafe label is triggered.
Safe examples include neutral everyday chat, normal Islamic practice, positive praise of Saudi leadership, ordinary Saudi daily-life complaints without leadership/royalty/legitimacy/holy-site/taboo triggers, neutral entertainment/friendship/celebrity content, clearly low-risk non-Saudi factual/academic/satirical political discussion, positive or neutral discussion of protected groups when no KSA local rule applies, neutral medical/scientific/sexual-health discussion, and emotions without abusive target, protected-identity attack, danger, political/security sensitivity, or other violation.

sample_quality:
Use trainable when the text is understandable, the evidence is clear, and the chosen final_label is directly supported by the text.
Use insufficient when the text is too short, corrupted, OCR-damaged, ambiguous, context-dependent, culturally implicit, controversial, or unstable for training.
Even when sample_quality is insufficient, final_label must still be the closest label and must not default to safe.
